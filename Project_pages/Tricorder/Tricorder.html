Tricorder

About the tricorder

I've been watching Star Trek since I was a kid and I always loved the different points of view on the world the series explains.
A point of view that is not explained but merely shown is the tehnical side of the sensors they use. One day I thought about what it woud feel like to use a hand-held scanner that could detect lots of things. A tricorder.

And I decided to build one.
I bought a toy tricorder to see how it feels and I LOVED IT.

The end goal of the project is a sensor array. It should measure:

  • Air temperature
  • Air humidity
  • Air pressure
  • Temperature of scanned object
  • Acceleration on x, y, z axis
  • GPS coordinates

NOTICE! The project is by no means ready, nor does it look ready. A tehnical problem with the power delivery circuits during the final assembly burned some essential components and I had to disassamble everything to trace the issue.

Mechanics

The chassis was modeled by me with the bought toy as a template.
I used a 3d designing software called "Blender", then printed the parts on an "Ender 3 pro" 3d printer with the help of "Cura" slicer.

Electronics

The electronics part consisted of choosing the sensors according to the voltage they use, testing all the parts on the breadboard and soldering for 3 days straight (only to cut everyting out afterwards).

Programing

Programing was not easy for this project due to the number of sensors used. A simple arduino nano does not have enough pins for all modules. Therefore I used 2 of them and had to make them communicate.
Even with 2 arduinos, the dynamic memory was very limited. The 1000+ lines of code and the numerous librarys use allmost all of the available RAM.
