About the CNC milling station

Seeing how long it takes to build a project, I began to look for ways to shorten that time. I reaslised I need to split the projects in two parts:

1. Design: I need to design all aspects of a project on a computer. That is because of the physical limitations that time and space impose on me. By achieving this I can use but one tool for any aspect of a project.

2. Production: After the design is ready and tested via simulations, it must be implemented in an automated way, so that it requires as little time as possible and can be repetable.

After researching methods for PCB production I chose milling because it requires no dangereous chemicals and most of the production process is automated.

The end goal of this project is a CNC milling station. Is should:

  • Have a high enough precision for milling DIP and SOIC packages
  • Have safety elements implemented (emergency button, electrical safetys, ...)
  • Be mobile (should have wheels for easy movement)
  • Have space for all tools required for producing PCB-s

Mechanical aspects

Compared to other projects that I worked on, this one consists mainly on designing and phisically building the CNC. So, this chapter of the description is the longest.

Design

I began working on the CNC in the spirit of the idea presented above: first design and then build. For mechanical design I used OnShape with the student account I have.
Designing the station took a rather long time, about 4 months. But, after these 4 months I was ready to build and the digital model of the station allowed me to measure any distance on any part. Building was much easier this way.

The chassis is made of alluminum profiles for strength and ease of assembly.
The more complex parts are made of wood because wood is easier to machine and I don't really have tools for working in metal. The only metal parts that I made myself are the mounts for the stepper motors, because the mounting type I used is not standard and I had to make my own mounts.

Mechanics

I designed the chassis in a 3d modeling program called "Blender" and printed it on an "Ender 3 Pro" with the help of "Cura" slicer. The chassis is made of two main parts held together by M3 screws.
The front holds the LCD and the buttons while the back holds the batteries.
