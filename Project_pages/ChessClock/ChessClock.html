Chess Clock

About the chess clock

At chess competitions, the two players use aspecial timer to measure the time left. This special timer actualy has 2 timers inside. When a player has made his move, he presses a button, his timer freezes and the opponets timer starts counting down.
The need for 2 separate timers and the fast switching between them renders the simple preinstalled phone timers useless.

Of course, there are apps with chess timers. "But", my friend Simon tought, "wouldn't an arduino clock be nicer?" I agreed and we started building the so called "Chess Clock".

The end goal of the project is a timer for chess competitons. It does:

  • Provide chess timer functionality
  • Provide simple timer functionality
  • Function as a digital dice (with 1 or 2 dice)
  • Run on batteries
  • Have an LCD display
  • Have LED and audio alerts

Electronics

Actualy soldering the cables for this project was harder than designing the circuit, mainly because we used an arduino Uno and we had to solder header pins to every wire.
By using a board for soldering the rest of the electronic components we made our job much, much easier.

Mechanics

I designed the chassis in a 3d modeling program called "Blender" and printed it on an "Ender 3 Pro" with the help of "Cura" slicer. The chassis is made of two main parts held together by M3 screws.
The front holds the LCD and the buttons while the back holds the batteries.
